Melamine Paper Quality Issues Practical Case Series 1 | WESTRONG-Optima™

Part 1: English Version (Original Technical Data)

I. Case Background

Target: 60g dark woodgrain melamine impregnated paper with silver powder.

  • Poor heat resistance: White irregular worm-like marks (scorch marks).
  • Demolding difficulty: Sticking to the steel plate.
  • Surface bubbles: Rice-sized bubbles appear after hot pressing.
  • Silver powder drift: Uneven distribution caused by resin impact.

II. Root Cause Analysis

DefectRoot Cause
Heat ResistanceSubstrate Mismatch (wood pulp fiber conflict) & Equipment Flaws (slow plate closing).
Sticking/BubblesPermeability Issues (reduced ink binder) & Physical Conflict in printing layer.
Silver DriftInsufficient Binder to fix silver powder under resin flow.

III. Solutions (Maxwell Yan's Core Logic)

  • Reduce Volatiles: Strictly lower volatile content within the paper.
  • Logical Timing: Alter resin curing logic (non-linear adjustment) rather than simply changing duration.
  • Enhanced Penetration: Improve resin penetration into base paper/ink layers, rather than just lowering viscosity.
