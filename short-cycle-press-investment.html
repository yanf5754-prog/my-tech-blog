📂 Investment Decision Logic

What is the priority for melamine board manufacturers when purchasing a short-cycle press? Different large-scale enterprises and owners will have different answers based on their philosophies. There are two types of enterprises in this industry:

  • Type A: Those entering the industry, comparing several suppliers by price, and buying the cheapest.
  • Type B: Those focused on the ratio of Capital Expenditure (CAPEX) to Output.

Maxwell's View: If I were purchasing a short-cycle press, which factors would I focus on?

1. Raw Board Logistics

The production line must automatically collect backing plates. After collecting 30 plates, they should be transferred by forklift to the end of the line for use as bottom plates, to be automatically picked up by the line. Some investors have high orders for 3-5 mm thin melamine boards. In this case, consider a transfer machine solution that picks up two 3-5 mm thin boards at once. Picking up two and pressing two at once significantly increases production efficiency for thin boards.

2. Melamine Paper Interaction

2.1 Automatic Paper Laying

This is determined by investment capacity and market tolerance. The advantage of automatic laying is that when the three elements—impregnated paper, board, and press—are perfectly aligned, production efficiency rises exponentially. The dwell time (pressing time) for ordinary melamine boards will be shortened to 8-10 seconds. In this scenario, manual paper laying cannot meet the efficiency requirements.

2.2 Manual Paper Laying

Currently the most mainstream solution in the market. If the elements of impregnated paper, board, and press cannot be aligned, efficiency will certainly be lower. Generally, the dwell time is 20-30 seconds. In this case, manual paper laying can reduce the CAPEX of equipment purchasing.

2.3 Thermal Physics of the Pressing Cycle

First, consider the uniformity of pressure and temperature in the press. The prerequisite for curing melamine paper resin is that the press increases the temperature of the impregnated paper, causing acidic substances in the resin to release and trigger curing. Another condition for curing is pressure; higher pressure leads to faster curing.

Physical Logic Formula:

Impregnated Paper Curing = f(Pressure, Temperature, Resin Curing Time, Volatile Content, Moisture Content, Temperature Uniformity)

Low-temperature areas on the hot plates cause uneven curing and deviations in glossiness. When the molecular elements are aligned, the dwell time directly determines production efficiency. Example: This is why customers of China's Qiangtong (Strongton) presses must first lower the press temperature when pressing dark colors. Qiangtong presses perform poorly in this regard; if the temperature is not lowered, the surface color will produce uneven patches, making it difficult to meet high-end customer requirements.
