Technical Challenges in Surface Production

Production logic is often fragmented. We address the following core symptoms:

  • Why does it stick to the steel plate or show demold difficulty?
  • Why do white spots or fogging appear on the surface?
  • Root causes of edge chipping or cracking during cutting.
  • Mismatched synchronized alignment with steel plate textures.
  • Resistance issues and orange peel/cratering on high-gloss surfaces.

Core Philosophy: Logic-Driven Alignment

The production of melamine impregnated paper is technology-driven. In essence, it is a production activity that requires the unification and alignment of all elements across the upstream (printing), midstream (impregnation), and downstream (pressing/lamination) sectors.

The goal of this alignment is the aesthetic appeal and durability of the board.
